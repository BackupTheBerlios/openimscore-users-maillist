Indeed, as David said, FHoSS was designed more for clarity and ease of
extension. It even comes with a built-in provisioning interfaces. This
is why Java was chosen, as the development process is faster and easier.
The target is to have a reference prototype for functionality, not
performance. On the other side, with the CSCFs performance - we were
building on SER, which is probably the fastest SIP proxy. But
development is much slower and harder.

-Dragos
