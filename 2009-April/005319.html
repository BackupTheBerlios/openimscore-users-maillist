> Hi Kavitha,

> I got an estimation from the MONSTER developers that a new release will 
> be available tomorrow, including a Linux version.
> 
> Then regarding your questions:
> 1) a client that does not do IPv6 can not connect to an IPv6-only IMS 
> core, of course. But most of the Java developments for example are 
> practically transparent and don't have issues, as long as no proprietary 
> IP address handling has been used in the sources.
> 2) yes. You can put multiple listening points to all the CSCFs and the 
> HSS. Yet I find this too be too big of a hassle, without a practical 
> result. Then you have to be careful because exclusive IPv4 or IPv6 CSCFs 
> can not talk to IPv6, respective IPv4 hosts.
> 
> In the end you will have to distinguish between the IPv4 and the IPv6 
> setup, so it is actually much easier to have non-mixed and parallel 
> running instances of the core. We will probably keep this "not-mixed" 
> setup for some time in our examples.
> 
> Cheers,
> -Dragos
