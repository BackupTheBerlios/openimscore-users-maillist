BTW: if you wonder what mt at xcscf.open-ims.test means - mt comes from 
mobile terminating and mo from mobile originating. So it is a little 
strange for the originating part, sending the ACK to follow first the 
terminating routes and then the originating ones... When a component 
receives this messages, based on the top-most Route header it knows if 
it should take care of the originating or terminating part. As we run in 
our little trials here just one P and one S, each do both jobs for both 
clients and this is part of the mechanism for finding out what should 
they do with that message.
