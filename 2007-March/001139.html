That's what I was saying all the time :). But I guess that the fact that
we had a SIP2IMS gateway meant that this was the way to do MD5
authentication. Well, since r178, the SIP2IMS gateway is history!

That means no more trouble when calling through the SIP2IMS Gateway :)

Another good news would be that I think that we could do an automatic
selection of the algorithm - if you don't present an Authorization
header in the first request, then the S-CSCF should do MD5 or
Early-IMS... I still have to figure a way to distinguish between these
two though...

In any case, if you want to use a specific scheme, just put in the first
Authorization header (no need to change the scscf.cfg).

-Dragos

Stefan.Brozinski at materna.de wrote:
> I have good news for everyone who uses sip2ims and experiences the 401
> problem.
>
>
> Solution: Don't use sip2ims anymore.
>
> 1. Configure your SIP client to connect to the P-CSCF on port 4060
> directly.
>
> 2. In the HSS, navigate to User Profiles / Private Identity /
> Authorization Scheme and change this from "Digest-AKAv1-MD5" to
> "Digest-MD5".
>
> 3. In scscf.cfg, find "MD5". Make sure the line that ends with "MD5" is
> active now and the line ending with "AKAv1-MD5" is commented out.
>
> Now you can connect X-Lite or whatever SIP client you like.
>
>
>
> Stefan
