Hello Mikael,

great and important question and thanks for raising that one. 
Everybody that uses this software in any other context than just for
internal test purposes, needs to be aware that there are MANY patents
issued in the field of IMS. 

A quick search for "ip multimedia subsystem" on a public IPR Search
Engine like www.depatisnet.de throws already 348 hits
(http://depatisnet.dpma.de/DepatisNet/depatisnet?window=1&space=main&content=einsteiger&action=treffer&session=c23b66f330d87d114f52f5db4bd0a72dfdc00dcebc4d&stamp=04917)

You can be sure that there are many more existing since the companies
that are active in the standardisation are securing their investments
they many for R&D and their work. One can think of software patents
whatever way you want, they are an issue in this context for sure.
Additionally, there are also many patents issued in the whole SIP/VoIP
domain and as well for Diameter, so be cautious on the topic.

Feel free to start searching for yourself in other databases like e.g.
www.uspto.gov (US patent office) or
http://www.european-patent-office.org/index.en.php

For the usage of the Open IMS Core we can only say, that the whole idea
of releasing it is based on fostering the adoption of IMS in testbeds
and for enabling tests of components that work around the core IMS
elements. We want stimulate an industry. For anybody who is trying to
build a commercial offer based on this code: be warned that some of the
patent owners are closely monitoring what we are doing here and probably
won't take this easy.

Hope this helps,

Peter
