That depends on what your AS does. I also used a dummy AS which properly
routed the requests back to the triggering S-CSCF. I have the cfg-file of
this AS attached.

> 
> 2.	Invite flows from  Ue1->pcscf->scscf->(AS)->scscf->pcscf->ue2
> .Is this right.

Yes. But to be precise there is an additional step because of the
"half-call" model. The scscf/mo (mobile originating) forwards the request
also to scscf/mt (mobile terminating), which is the same mode in our case.

So I expect the INVITE flows:
Ue1->pcscf/mo->scscf/mo->(AS)->scscf/mo->scscf/mt->pcscf/mt->ue2

I have also attached a trace protocol (plus scenario-trace) from a test,
where I involved 2 AS (one on an originating trigger, and one on a
terminating trigger). Have a look at the call flow.
