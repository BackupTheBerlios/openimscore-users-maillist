No, the setup is different. Our registrars do not re-use the usrloc. Nor
do they have a similar db dump functionality.

However, the persistency support replaces that. Mario and me have been
working on persistency support and now you can configure the P/S-CSCF to
dump data in the database (Mario's work, still in progress for the
P-CSCF) or on files (my part). You can configure the interval at which
the data is dumped and also another dump is done if you terminate the
CSCFs nicely. Then on any start-up the data is reloaded. This covers the
registrars as well as other data, like the dialog tables or the stored
authentication vectors.

I did just periodic full dumps, while Mario is working also on
incremental saves.

The result is that you can now restart the CSCFs without big loses - you
still loose the transactional states.
