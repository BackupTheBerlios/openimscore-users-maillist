Hi Arnold,
 
> Actually my problem was, that I had one port number in the UDP header, and
> another one in the Via and Contact-headers. Because of the "rport"
> parameter, the P-CSCF sent the reply always to the port number in the UDP.
> Which than did not come to my client, since it prefers the number in the
> Via and the Contact-header...

O.k. that's how rport works. Fine. But the rest should be done by your NAT
device. It should translate the "rport"-port back to the port used in the
Via and the Contact-header.

Please check you NAT device. It should work in both directions!
