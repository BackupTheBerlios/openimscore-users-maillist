:) well, IMS is actually an architecture recommendation. It is based on
IETF protocols. The good thing is that it standardizes these "functions"
that have to offer these "interfaces" which are nothing more than a
bunch of IETF RFCs put together. And this is the greatest thing - if in
VoIP you would be happy if just the damn RTP stream is set-up, here you
have to bother about all the rest of the stuff.

In conclusion, I agree with you: if you read the standards, there is no
difference between being IMS compliant and having a good and compliant
IETF SIP/Diameter node. However, looking back at how badly the standards
are actually implemented, it is good to be arrogant and mandate so many
things in IMS in order to accomplish even the simplest tasks.

-Dragos
