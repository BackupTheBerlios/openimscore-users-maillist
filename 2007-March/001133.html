I am not ruling out X-Lite just because it is not Open Source but
because I do not think that we should adapt IMS for SIP but SIP to IMS
and we can't do that with X-Lite. It is not our target to have a core
that would inter-work with SIP clients that do not have the required
functionality. Or?

As I previously said in another thread, we are discontinuing support for
the SIP2IMS Gw. It might be broken in the latest revisions and I for one
do not see the benefit of fixing it when I can do MD5 authentication
without it. Attached you have a trace of a successful MD5 authentication
without it.

Unfortunately I can't run this with X-Lite because the latest versions
are windows only and the trouble of running it is too big, compared to a
SIPp script.

-Dragos
