Dear Dragos,

IMS AKA is UICC-based since the UICC is a main asset in 3GPP and 3GPP MNOs seem quite happy about it.

At the beginning TISPAn was willing to adopt the UICC for the IMS, then it became evident that probably this will not happen in the (near?) future, despite the claims and then IMS authentication mechanisms like SIP digest and NASS bundled, that are "alternatives" to the IMS AKA has been somehow added to the TS 33.203.

Initially they were documented as individual IMS authentication mechanisms, then we realized that the coexistance of all possible standard IMS Authentication mechanisms had to be guaranteed, in some way (and then the Annex P was specified, in the same Rel-8).



Referring to the backward compatibility issue, I guess that it can be guaranteed as long as such a requirement is put forward by the interested parties... Providing backward compatibility against proprietary solutions or against solutions that 3GPP is not even aware of seems to be nearly impossible... Isn't it?



I might be wrong, however in my view a solution that is not expected by a certain standard is proprietary, in respect of the same standard (then the same solution can be absolutely standard in another standardization body and maybe also for other purposes, but this is another story).

Note that I am not saying that a certain proprietary solution is bad, but only that is proprietary, at least in the context where it is used. As far as I understand OpenIMSCore aims to consider also possible proprietary solutions, if deemed useful/appropriate. If this is the case, I am fine about it (even if I am a little bit surprised because at the very end proprietary solutions might result being much more numerous than the ones agreed by a certain standard, that is 3GPP for IMS).



Referring to the possible improvements to the 3GPP standard, well, with n.4 (already!!) alternative IMS authentication mechanisms (PLUS one coexisting mechanism to allow them coexisting) I would hope to see something working in the field, prior suggesting possible improvements/enhancements...;o)))

In my view to have something working IN PRACTICE, we need a stable standard, and Rel-8 has been finalized only some months ago..., so honestly I would be reluctant in suggesting whatsover improvement, at this stage... Maybe we need some corrections... ;o))



The coexistance is a critical issue in the sense that in a common IMS scenario there could be some users authenticating to a certain S-CSCF with SIP digest and others authenticating to the SAME S-CSCF with IMS AKA. Without the coexistance mechanism, how can the S-CSCF know how to behave in the various scenarios?

Referring to the fact that "Maybe some new users would want to make authorizations based entirely on PKI and TLS (or whatever). I don't think that we should deny them the chance of configuring a particular S-CSCF"...., well I am not saying that this is prevented by the standard 3GPP or that it has to be prevented, however a standard where everybody is allowed to do whatever he/she wants is not really a standard anymore, in my view. Rather, a standard should try to single out a set of possibilities that are perceived to cover all or most of the represented parties (of course parties that do not attend the meetings cannot pretend having their needs fully taken into account by the standard....;o)))



Finally, in general the latest version of TS 33.203 is available here: http://www.3gpp.org/ftp/Specs/html-info/33203.htm (and the latest Rel-8 version is the 8.6.0 one.)



Thanks

Regards,

Mauro

I see the advantages of AKA over just MD5, but I understand the

reluctance. Why have such a high entrance threshold for doing anything

with IMS, especially in our experimentation targets? Sure, you can do

AKA in software, but this should be discouraged as it practically kills

the AKA purpose.

>

> The Common IMS process (started within the 3GPP Rel-8) was decided to

> accommodate the IMS needs coming from various players and as it has be

> a real nightmare to specify, now I hope it will be helpful for the

> implementors... ;o))

>

I hope too. But I also hope that it is backwards compatible and easy to

extend to. So this might mean a +4 new methods in the worst case.

Otherwise it will be a nightmare to implement and support. And unlike

the standardization bodies, we can't just ignore for example Rel.6

compatible components.

>

> If IMS authentication mechanisms other than those considered by TS

> 33.203 Rel-8 are left in the OpenIMSCore website it could be fine, I

> guess, however I cannot figure out how they will be handled IN

> PRACTICE by the "coexistence mechanism" described within the Annex P

> of TS 33.203 Rel-8, provided that the coexistance mechanism will be

> implemented in the future by the OpenIMCcore website.

>

I see it as working for a subset. Maybe we'll just mark somehow the

coexisting ones in the web interface. Anyway, I would be against

limiting the Open IMS Core functionality to what the standards say. It

is a good idea that the default configuration would use the standard

recommended ones, but I believe that our users should have the choice.

>

> In my view the standard can be used as a guideline as long as you

> comply with it, but if you start deviating (e.g. adding options that

> might be absolutely reasonable but that are not foreseen by the

> standard), then there is the chance that you cannot rely anymore on

> the conclusions/guidelines written in the standard (in my view, as a

> minimum you have to doublecheck everything, as you never know, that

> means wasting all the efforts done to write the standard...).

>

true... yet I would rather let the others in. I hope that this way

someone would figure out new solutions and try them, then improve the

standards.



And anyway, is coexistence such a critical item? Maybe some new users

would want to make authorizations based entirely on PKI and TLS (or

whatever). I don't think that we should deny them the chance of

configuring a particular S-CSCF.

>

yes

it needs to be checked and aligned
