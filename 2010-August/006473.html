That is much better. I have quickly created a scenario filew where you can
easily see what happens (2 files in zip, extract them and click on
tracefilephonetopc_index.html).

Combined with the tracefile (pcap) you can see the problem again in packets
26 and 27. After receiving the 180 Ringing with SDP the Nokia sends an RTP
packet (packet 26). It is not clear why it does this, maybe to check
connectivity. 
This packet is correctly sent from 192.168.102.63:49152 to
192.168.102.12:20505 but an ICMP error comes back from 192.168.102.12:49152
to 192.168.102.63:20505. This ICMP error packet (sent by UCT IMS client) is
wrong: the portnumbers are totally intermingled. It has received the RTP
packet correctly (can be verified in the payload of packet 27).

It is unclear why UCT IMS sends an ICMP error. May be it odes not expect an
RTP packet before session is setup fully (200 OK to INVITE). But then it
responds with an erroneous ICMP packet.

The Nokia phone does not see any sense to continue session setup in that
case.
