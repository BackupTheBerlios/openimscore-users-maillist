Hi,

I have a question about routing of non-dialog initiating messages like OPTIONS. When a client application sends OPTIONS, it includes a Route-header pointing to the assigned S-CSCF. OPTIONS is received at P-CSCF and I expect the message to goto S-CSCF, however, it's instead routed to I-CSCF, then to the S-CSCF. For the INVITE, it goes from P-CSCF to S-CSCF. Not sure why it's not honoring the Route-header and sending it to I-CSCF first.

Also, it looks like I-CSCF is asking HSS where the destination (To-header) is registered. I think the spec says that I-CSCF should be asking HSS where source (From-header) is registered. I have same source sending OPTIONS to different destinations and OPTIONS is routed correctly to the registered destination, but rejected by I-CSCF when destination is not registered. So, I think it's checking the destination.

Basically, I want to route all OPTIONS to my Application Server and have a trigger point defined in S-CSCF… but because of this routing issue, it's not even getting to S-CSCF. What causes OPTIONS to goto I-CSCF instead? Is there a workaround?
