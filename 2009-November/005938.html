> Hi Gemi,
>
> the entire IMS security architecture was built so that there won't be a need
> anymore for things like the 407 or any 401 for other than an initial
> authentication. So although what you propose would be possible, it is very
> disruptive for the IMS platform. You can of course look in the S-CSCF code
> on how the REGISTER request is challenged and so on and adapt that for
> INVITE.
>
> But in the end, what is the benefit of challenging a user that was already
> authenticated before it hit the S-CSCF?
>
> BTW: Here I assume that CSCF #1 is the S-CSCF, as the P-CSCF from a visited
> network won't ever have access to the HSS in the user's home domain.
>
> Cheers,
> -Dragos
