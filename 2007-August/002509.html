>     it seems that ACK does not indicate correct Route header.
>
>     As it is a different transaction within same dialog, it should
>     copy route header as indicated in 180 ringing. Without it ,
>     routeing will not be possible.
>

>
>
>     	
>     	
>

>
>
>     I am trying to test a scenario where the AS initiates a session
>     towards a registered user. It does not work ...
>
>     The schematics of the scenario is:
>     1. AS sends INVITE to Bob to S-CSCF
>     2. S-CSCF performs terminating services for Bob (no iFC configured)
>     3. INVITE reaches Bob through P-CSCF
>     4. Bob answers with 180 Ringing and 200OK
>     5. 180 Ringing and 200OK reach the AS, through Proxy and Serving CSCF
>     6. AS ACK's to Bob sending ACK to Service CSCF
>     7. Serving CSCF does not forward the ACK to Bob
>
>     The Serving CSCF fails to recognize the ACK as being subsequent to
>     an ongoing terminating dialog and does not forward it to Bob.
>     Session fails.
>
>     Find attached HTML scenario and a pcap capture. I am using sipp to
>     simulate both the AS and Bob.
>
>     I think the root cause is that the Serving CSCF is not
>     Record-Route'ing itself towards the AS in the 180Ringing or 200OK.
>     So the AS does not know how to mark the ACK so it is appropriately
>     recognized as belonging to a subsequent dialog.
>
>     Thoughts?
