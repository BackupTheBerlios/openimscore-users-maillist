Hi Alok,
 
> if there is one Public identity e.g  carol at open-ims.test is it possible to
create
> multiple SIP URI public identities like 
> sip:1000 at open-ims.test
> sip:1001 at open-ims.test 
> all mapped to single public identity.?

There are some misunderstandings in above statement.
- carol at open-ims.test is not a public identity. It has the structure of a
private identity (no "sip:" schema).
- Public identities cannot be mapped to other public identities, but only to
private identities.

> Can they call can between them ?

A private identity is bound to a terminal. If the public identities are
bound to the same terminal, it does not make sense (but it may work if the
terminal is capable of having more than one session at the same time). If
they are mapped to different terminals it will work, but it is still not a
realistic use case.

> if someone can give some use cases of multiple
public identities ,specifically
> in relation with Openimscore to test that will great.

Multiple public identities allow a person to have different user profiles
for different identities on the same terminal.
Example: You may have a diversion to voice mail outside of business hours on
your business identity, but you may be still reachable for your friends on
another identity.
