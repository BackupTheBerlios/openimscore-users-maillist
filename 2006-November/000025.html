Hi Outi,

Don't despair, I am compiling a FAQ and I will upload it soon. This is 
one of those and at the workshop this was a point on a slide.

You need the ipsec-tools package installed (at least this is how it is 
called in Gentoo Linux). The setkey is a utility that manipulates the 
Linux kernel 2.6 IPSec implementation. You probably also need your 
kernel compiled with AH and ESP for IPSec if you plan to actually use 
the IPSec tunnels (if not, just comment the 2 lines in the pcscf.sh and 
forget about it).

The solution was simple, you are right, but once you do it on one 
machine you forget about it.

-Dragos
