Hi Viral,

if you filter your wireshark capture with "tcp.port == 3868" you've got
the HSS port, and the same with 3869 (icscf) and 3870 (scscf). (the
ports they use to listen the diameter messages)

then you have diameter messages and if you click right on one of them
and you choose Decode As... and transport label and then DIAMETER, you
will see the diameter messages. (three times, one each port)

after that you can filter again the first capture with "sip or diameter"
and you'll see both kind of messages.

that is the way i do, i hope it will be useful for you

regards,
Enrique
