Hi Arun,

my answer inlined:

Venkatesh Arun-A21466 wrote:
> Do u mean PCSCF implements NAT travesal mechanisms?? If yes can u pls
> let me understand how and wat configuration is required to work for the
> setup like the below (test clients behind NAT).?? 
>   
please look in the pcscf.cfg. The configuration and commands are marked 
with "NAT" ;-).

> >From your mail, it means to me like we **SHOULD NOT** register with
> public IP (10.x.x.x) in the first place?? Correct??
>   
first of all, 10.x.x.x is not a public IP, but anyway. You should 
register the contact that you're going to use, as in the one that you'll 
send in the Via header. There should be no SIP proxies between the 
client and the P-CSCF and the P-CSCF concept in IMS assumes that you do 
control the Access Network, so NAT should not really be a problem.

> If the answer for the above is yes, then If registratons happen with
> private ips, will PCSCF takes care of mapping AOR(or registerd contact)
> to public ip address from where the registration request received?? Or
> how this mapping is takenn care of in order to route the incoming
> requests?
>   
it's called a NAT pinhole and it is maintained alive in the P-CSCF. Be 
aware though that you can't have both IPSec and NAT in the P-CSCF (at 
least for now).
