I am teaching SIP and IMS at the University of Applied Sciences in Vienna
(http://www.technikum-wien.at/) as a lecturer. There I have lectures and
seminars and I am also tutor for bachelor and master theses.

My main job is at Alcatel-Lucent in Vienna in the area of IMS. But as there
is no IMS-development department in Vienna my job is more technical sales
oriented.

At the university I recommend to the students to use OpenIMSCore for
experimenting and to deepen knowledge on IMS. Unfortunately this does not
always happen as much as I expect.

Actually there are two master-theses ongoing using OSIMS as a platform.
These theses both deal with application development using SIP servlets.

In the last semester I had 
- a bachelor theses on integrating a presence server (OpenSER) into IMS
- a diploma thesis on developing the Rf interface for OSIMS (unfortunately
not finished)
- a master thesis on security algorithms (Ciphering Protocols and
Authentication Methods) in IMS

In the near future there will be
- a seminar work on PCC (investigation and evaluation)
- a seminar work on developing a trace-tool for SIP- and diameter flows
- some more students doing master theses on application development 

As a short summary: OpenIMSCore is a perfect platform to study IMS. Without
practical lessons it would be a rather dry theory. I whish I could get more
students willing to write some code as a contribution.

Thanks to the team at FOKUS!

Franz
