You are invited to attend the upcoming 7th Emergency Services Workshop from May 11 - 13, 2010.

The Emergency Services Workshop aims to foster the development and deployment of standards-based systems for next-generation, IP-based emergency calling.

Timely topics of the workshop include:

- Current issues related to standards for emergency calling
- Architectures and deployment experiences from different jurisdictions around the world (including national, state, and local levels)
- Prototypes of standards-based IP emergency services
- Authority-to-citizen alerts and early warning
- Automatic IP geolocation technologies and deployment considerations
- Implementation and deployment reports.

As part of this Workshop, Telcordia will be demonstrating its latest prototype efforts in IMS-based location-based services capabilities for First Responders to emergencies. This prototype includes implementations of the Open Mobile Alliance LOCSIP specifications and 3GPP IMS Emergency Services specifications.

The host for the workshop will be the MAXWell Laboratory at the University of Maryland, located in College Park, Maryland<http://maps.google.com/maps/ms?ie=UTF&msa=0&msid=116511782097883576773.000483d4e0dee0965150a>, a northern suburb of Washington, DC, USA.

For workshop registration, go here<https://intranet.umiacs.umd.edu/conferences/esc2010/reg.htm>.

For further information on the workshop, go here<http://www.emergency-services-coordination.info/esw7.html>.

I hope to see you at the 7th Emergency Services Workshop.
