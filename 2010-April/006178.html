> Could you please tell me, if the password is the same in client and server
> side, and the IMPU (sip:darvasipeter at open-ims.test) and IMPI
> (darvasipeter) ((is it legal to name IMPI like this?)) are set and linked,
> and the client supports MD5, how can the following trace occur?

No. It is not allowed to name the IMPI in that way. Look at TS 23.228
§4.3.3.1 Private User Identities

The private identity is assigned by the home network operator, and used, for
example, for Registration, Authorisation, Administration, and Accounting
purposes. This identity shall take the form of a Network Access Identifier
(NAI) as defined in IETF RFC 2486 [14].
