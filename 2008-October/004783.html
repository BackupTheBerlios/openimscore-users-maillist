On Fri, Oct 24, 2008 at 12:43 AM, Franz Edler <franz.edler at inode.at> wrote:

> Hi Rishikesh,
>
> Looking at the Trigger Point you are using I found that the Trigger Point
> (shown below) can never match. You have to use Disjunctive Normal Form
> instead of Conjunctive Normal Form. A request can never be a REGISTER and a
> SUBSCRIBE at the same time!
>
> Your trigger point is:
>
> <TriggerPoint>
> <ConditionTypeCNF>1</ConditionTypeCNF>
> <SPT>
> <ConditionNegated>0</ConditionNegated>
> <Group>0</Group>
> <Method>REGISTER</Method>
> <Extension>
> </Extension>
> </SPT>
> <SPT>
> <ConditionNegated>0</ConditionNegated>
> <Group>0</Group>
> <Method>SUBSCRIBE</Method>
> <Extension>
> </Extension>
> </SPT>
> <SPT>
> <ConditionNegated>0</ConditionNegated>
> <Group>1</Group>
> <SIPHeader>
> <Header>Event</Header>
> <Content>*reg*</Content>
> </SIPHeader>
> <Extension>
> </Extension>
> </SPT>
> </TriggerPoint>
> <ApplicationServer>
> <ServerName>sip:127.0.0.1:5060</ServerName>
> <DefaultHandling>1</DefaultHandling>
> </ApplicationServer>
> </InitialFilterCriteria>
> </ServiceProfile>
> </IMSSubscription>
>
>
> I suggest you should change <ConditionTypeCNF>1</ConditionTypeCNF> to
> <ConditionTypeCNF>0</ConditionTypeCNF>.
>
> -Franz
>
