but the SIP URI of the AS is the one stored in the HSS... So you only 
need to change it there, not in the actual AS. And this extra parameter 
in the first Route header pointing to the AS should not be a reason for 
the AS to refuse it.

The UDP/TCP automatic detection in SER puzzled me too... I am not sure 
about it though as I never had to look too closely at it and I always 
could force it.
