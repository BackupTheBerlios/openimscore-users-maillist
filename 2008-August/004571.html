Hi Eduardo,
 
> ... I've made a capture with tcpdump and this is what I get:

For tcpdump I usually use the command:
   tcpdump -i any -s 3000 -w /var/tmp/capture.pcap not port 22 and not port
3306
This command avoids to capture SSH and MySQL packets but captures everything
else which is interesting for diagnostics (including fragmented packets),
especially SIP and diameter traffic.

> By the way, what do you mean by "active trigger point"? I guess that
> you're saying that something is missing in the client configuration and
> therefore packets can not reach the destiny (the two clients are installed
> in a different subnet than the one where the openIMSCore is).

No, it is nothing on the client side. I mean the openimscore platform
forwards the matching SIP requests to an application server. If you do not
have an appropriate application server in place you will get a destination
unreachable response.
