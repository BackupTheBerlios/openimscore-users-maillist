Hi all,

I am going to spam you a bit with one of our non-free products. I hope
that you won't be too annoyed by this and maybe you will find it
actually useful. Also, needless to say, this product is one of the many
Open-IMS-Playground at FOKUS components that sponsor a lot of the Open
Source IMS Core development work, so here's "a word from our sponsor"
;-). Also you can take a look on the attached flyer and of course,
http://sipnuke.org


*SIPNuke* is the flexible NGN load generator developed at the Fraunhofer
FOKUS <http://www.fokus.fraunhofer.de/> institute. Its key features are
*performance*, *flexibility* and *ease of use*.

We, at FOKUS <http://www.fokus.fraunhofer.de/>, have a very long R&D
experience with SIP <http://www.iptel.org> and IMS
<http://www.openimscore.org>. And while we worked on this, we had a
constant need for better tools: faster, easier to use, more flexible and
easily extensible in the future.

FOKUS
	

Regardless of what you do with SIP or NGN, you always need a powerful,
flexible, fast and easy to use test tool. SIPNuke was developed such
that you could stop wasting time on testing your
prototype/product/solution and keep concentrating on your core business.
We encourage you to read more about it and try it. A trial version is
given for free and should allow you to see that what we claim it can do
is real. We assure you that a full license won't cost a fortune and if
you still think it does, we'll try and tailor a special license just for
you.

Also here we will try to create a SIPNuke user community that would
share a repository of tests and best-practices as big as it can be. All
those scenarios are free for you to take directly, study, change to your
specific needs and run inside SIPNuke Our community's scope is to free
up the time that you now use for designing, writing, validating and
running tests, and instead let you concentrate on your core business.

Some of the currently most used scenarios are:

    *

      We are maintaining an implementation of the DTS/TISPAN-06027 NGN
      Interoperability test specification, published in April 2008,
      consisting of 70+ related tests or analysing basic IMS
      functionality. Visit the ETSI Task Force
      <http://portal.etsi.org/stfs/STF_HomePages/STF328/STF328.asp>
      website for more information.

    *

      SIPNuke is the base for an implementation of the ETSI/TISPAN IMS
      Benchmarking specification, ETSI TS 186 008 published in February
      2008. The document
      <http://pda.etsi.org/pda/home.asp?wki_id=5iXh_xoTFEWYcZdeMBqbi>
      (PDF) is available from etsi.org <http://etsi.org>.

Evaluation licenses for trial purposes are provided for free. Further
license levels are available, as well as new on-request features and
additional technical support for defining and running new test suites.
