Hello Yogi,

I guess it is enough.

But I think that you do not want to do that. Unless you are sending
ALWAYS an Authorization header in the REGISTER message (which I think
that you don't in the first REGISTER) with a good private identity.

The reason is that the private and public identities are different
things. The private is used only for authentication and in case that you
do not ALWAYS specify it, the CSCFs still have a way of dealing with
non-IMS SIP clients. This is done be removing the scheme and the
parameters from the public identity URI. So if you have old non-IMS
clients and the public identity is sip:alice at open-ims.test you will want
to stick to private identity alice at open-ims.test.

-Dragos
