Hi Peter,

Thanks for your answer!

Since the ISIM is as such an application on the SIM card, some of the main 
sim vendors are offering the opportunity to operators to put the ISIM as 
part of their SIM profile (it only involves putting the new files with 
keys and identities etc.). Thats the least of problems as I see it (from 
an operator point of view).

However, for someone to make use of it and test it with for example the 
open-ims test bed, or any other IMS infrastructure, I guess you need to 
have a device which reads the ISIM. Otherwise the original tamper-proof 
AKA framework that was supposed to mimic 3G authentication for higher 
security in IMS is pretty much lost. Am I incorrect in the previous 
statement?

What about the Open IMS Client FOKUS is developing for symbian, smartphone 
etc? Does it use the ISIM?

As I see it, its pretty much a chicken-egg situation. Vendors seem to be 
reluctant to start interoperating with the ISIM and operators are not 
ready to get an ISIM enabled UICC before they can see if it works on a 
device. Is the original ISIM approach being discarded?

regards,
Josef

I don't think that there are too many devices yet out there that can
claim fully tested IMS support for the integration of ISIMs, mainly due
to the fact that AFAIK no ISIMs are widely used yet with operators (any
updates on this are welcome ;).

The solutions we know mostly helped themselves by using a software
solution rather than relying on a UICC to do the authentication for
them.
