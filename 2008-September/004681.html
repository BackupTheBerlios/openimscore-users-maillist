Franz Edler wrote:
> Hi Pedro,
>
>   
>> Is this behaviour normal?
>>     
>
> Yes. The terminating user may have subscribed to services for unregisterd
> users (e.g. forwarding to voicemail) it needs an S-CSCF to handle it.
> Therefore an S-CSCF has to be assigned. For further details see 3GPP TS
> 23.228 § 5.12.1.
>
>   
and to the original remark, the originating S-CSCF does not choose the
S-CSCF, but the terminating I-CSCF chooses the terminating S-CSCF, if
none is already assigned. Look closely on the first traces, before the
user makes it to the unregistered state.
>> How can we avoid this behaviour?
>>     
>
> Not at all.
>   
actually you can, but only if you do not have for that user any services
for the unregistered case. That means that all your IFCs have the
Profile Part Indicator to "Registered".
