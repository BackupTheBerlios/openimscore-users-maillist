Hi Wathana,

 

Not sure what the IMS Forum is, do you mean the Third Generation Partnership
Project (3GPP)?

 

In that case, the relationship is like this: 3GPP is a global forum that
produces Technical Specifications, including core network functionality such
as IMS. Notice that 3GPP is not a standardization body. See
http://www.3gpp.org/About/about.htm for further information on 3GPP.

 

ETSI, on the other hand, is a standardization body – for Europe (i.e., it is
a regional standardization body). They are one of the partners of the 3GPP
collaboration. Other partners of 3GPP are ARIB, ATIS and others.

 

After 3GPP has created a specification the standardization bodies
standardizes them for their region, sometimes slightly modifying the specs.

 

In [1] you’ll find a much better description of the relationship between
these and also IETF. 

 

[1] G. Camarillo and M. A. García-Martín, The 3G IP multimedia subsystem
(IMS) : merging the Internet and the cellular worlds, 2nd ed. Chichester:
Wiley, 2006.
http://www.amazon.com/3G-IP-Multimedia-Subsystem-IMS/dp/other-editions/B001C
BAX80/ref=dp_ed_all
