I think that the specific concern was that early deployments won't
benefit from USIM/ISIM ready SIM cards in the terminals. I have some
cards like that and the benefits are huge, but you probably still don't
get them from operators. Back then, only AKA was defined for
authentication in 3GPP, so this was quickly hacked-in. Strange, as the
MD5 was long time present in the so much similar RFC(draft) 4740 on the
Diameter SIP Application.

Cheers,
-Dragos

Klemens Schragel wrote:
> If you want to know more about early IMS have a look at TR 33.978. In short terms early IMS considers the fact, that in the first days of IMS not every UE will be ably to use the required authentication methods. so there is a binding made at Access network plane (SGSN, GGSN), which is then communicated to the signalling plane (IMS plane). The idea is the same as for NASS-bundles-auth, only that Early-IMS is focused on mobile access networks and nass-bundled- auth on wireline access networks...
