Hi Community!

Asterisk has a lot of really good functionalities. For instance, Asterisk
can communicate with 3G networks if you install a ISDN card (
http://www.voip-info.org/wiki/view/Asterisk+H324M).
Asterisk can also transcode video over Internet (or 3G if you enable that
card) in h263 (or h263+). Thus, it proposes services such Video IVR, Video
monitoring (using RTSP IP cameras)
I think we have to find a way to interconnect openIMSCore with Asterisk.
if you need help, just contact me...

Khaldon

2008/7/28 Dragos Vingarzan <Dragos.Vingarzan at fokus.fraunhofer.de>

> I think that Asterisk is great! Actually it's one of the big SIP open
> source products out there.
>
> However, I don't think that it is either targeted at handling millions
> of users, rather hundreds to thousands. Or for that matter to trigger
> services in the IMS sense.
>
> And here is where our Open IMS Core comes in place - in theory it should
> be able to handle all those millions of users starving for services, but
> not the services ;-). It does not have all the bells and whistles that
> Asterisk has (actually, it's just about signaling and no media), so
> complementing it with Asterisk as a "processor" or PABX-replacement
> would have many advantages.
>
> Cheers,
> -Dragos
