Hi,

 

It is not clear to me what you are asking about here, in addition to what you assume about the core network:

-          What is the network ID? Open-ims.test or iptv/vod.open-ims.test?

-          Do you have a wildcard PSI for channelX@ setup?

-          What do you think is different with this request as compared to “INVITE sip:alice@ open-ims.test”? Or, said in a different way: What is special with this request as compared to “INVITE sip:alice at open-ims.test”?

 

Basically, the OpenIMS CN does not care whether the request is to me (sip:nobody at open-ims.test) or IPTV (sip:iptv at open-ims.test). If there is any iFC or PSI involved it will take care of that to route it to the right place, but not so much more. It’s the service that needs to make the session valuable… 

 

PS: You might be interested in checking out the ETSI TISPAN IPTV standardization for details here. For example ETSI TS 183 063 NGN-R2 “MS-based IPTV stage 3 specification” (http://www.tech-invite.com/Ti-tispan-standards-b.html#ts-183-063) and ETSI TS 182 028 NGN-R2 “IPTV Architecture; Dedicated subsystem for IPTV functions” (http://www.tech-invite.com/Ti-tispan-standards.html#ts-182-028) <http://www.tech-invite.com/Ti-tispan-standards.html#ts-182-028%29> ... And related documents. Moreover, [1] gives more practical details on IPTV in NGN.
