I hope some more things are clear...

port-c is for the UAC case - that means - requests sent from the UE
*and* responses for that request received by the UE

port-s is for the UAS case - that means - requests sent to the UE *and*
responses that the UE sends out for that.

So each port is bidirectional, in the spirit of RFC3261. The c/s referes
to UAC/UAS and not client/server in the sense of who sends the message
and who receives it.

AND!!! The IPSec is not compatible with NAT! There is one spec somewhere
that deals with that, but we do not support it.
