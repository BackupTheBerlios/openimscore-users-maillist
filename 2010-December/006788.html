Hello All!

Franz, not exactly.

The issue occured always, when I use Monster client. Of course I tried other
local ports, but the problem came forward in every case.

For example, with local port 27752, I got:

5(8366) INF:P-CSCF:----------  Registrar Contents begin --------
 5(8366) INF:P-CSCF:[ 248] C:
<0://[2001:738:2001:20a0:2e0:ff:fe9b:32b0:27752]:5060> Exp:[3548] R:[ 1]
SOS:[ ] <sip:test4@[2001:738:2001:20a0:2e0:ff:fe9b:32b0:27752]>

So the client added the local port to the end of the ipv6 address of the
client and it can be seen in the Contact field of the SIP message, too (you
can see it in the attached capture file).


But in the meantime, I wrote a mail directly to the Monster developer team
with this issue, and they found a mistake in creating the contact header (in
the Monster). I got a fixed jar file, which solves the problem. Of course,
it will be in the next release of the client.

Best regards:
Gergő
