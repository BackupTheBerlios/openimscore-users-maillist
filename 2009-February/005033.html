Zdenek,
 
> I need transmit voice in high quality (16b, 44kHz, without compression)
> from
> client A to client B and simultaneously store it for latter processing. Is
> there such a codec and is it possible to implement it to the client? I
> would
> like to use for it OpenIMS. Is it a good idea? Does an application exist
> for
> OpenIms, which works as a recorder?

The quality of a voice session is rather a question of the IMS terminal and
not of IMS core. Recording might be offered by both the terminal and by an
IMS application.

For a simple approach I suggest to use a client which supports your
requirments. Eybeam from http://www.counterpath.com/ supports both
requirements (broadband voice codec and recording).
