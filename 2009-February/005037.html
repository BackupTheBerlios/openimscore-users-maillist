no problem!

...well, sips-uri indicates, that YOUR domain (originating) shall use tls for each hop, the handling within the destination domain is...say "non of your business". if someone addresses your uri with sips it means just, that the transport to the first node of your domain is tls secured, the rest within your domain is not forced to use tls.

unfortunately I don't know anything about sips in IMS, all security issues are covered by other mechanisms (see 33.203 about Access Security (first hop) and 33.210 about Network Domain Security (domain internal and inter-domain)..
